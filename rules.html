Dice Poker

Equipment:

  • Three standard dice for each player and two communal dice.
  • A cup for each player.

Game Setup:

  • Determine the order of play. You can either agree on a specific order or use a random method, such as rolling dice to determine the first player.
  • Each player starts with an equal amount of chips or agreed-upon currency.

Objective:

  • The goal is to have the highest-ranking poker hand at the end of the game (see below for hand rankings).

Gameplay:

  • The game consists of two rounds of betting and two dice rolls.
  • Before each round, players place an agreed-upon initial bet into the pot.
  • After each round, the player to the left of the first player becomes the new first player.

First Round:

  1. Each player rolls their three dice privately.
  2. Bets are made, starting with the first player.
  3. Each player can either match the bet of the previous player, or fold, meaning they withdraw from the game.

Second Round:

  1. The first player rolls one communal dice so that all players can see.
  2. Players combine the communal die with their own to make a hand.
  3. The round of betting is repeated.

Showdown:

  • The first player rolls the remaining communal die.
  • Players use this to complete their hand.
  • The final round of betting is repeated.
  • Remaining players reveal their hands and the winner takes all money in the pot.

Hand Rankings (Highest to lowest):

  1. Five-of-a-Kind — All fice dice of the same value.
  2. Four-of-a-Kind — Four dice of the same value.
  3. Full House — A pair of one value and a three-of-a-kind of another.
  4. Six High Straight — Dice with sequential values from two through six.
  5. Five High Straight — Dice with sequential values one to fice.
  6. Three-of-a-kind — Three dice of the same value.
  7. Two Pairs — Two pairs of dice, each with the same value.
  8. Pair — Two dice with the same value.
  • The winner of hands of the same kind is determined by the higher value of die e.g. a pair of sixes beats a pair of fours.
  • In the event of two players having a full house, add the values of the players hands, with the highest determining the winner e.g. player one has a pair of twos and a three-of-a-kind with fives, equalling 19. Player two has a pair of sixes and a three-of-a-kind with ones, equalling 15. Player one wins.
  • Ties are settled by a split pot.
